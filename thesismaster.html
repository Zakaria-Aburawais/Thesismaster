Reading between the lines…
The examination method

Judged the way real examiners judge.

Every review and audit runs against the criteria committees actually use — not a generic grammar check.

DIM · ARGUMENT

Defensibility

Is the central claim precise, contestable, and carried through every chapter — or does it drift?

DIM · EVIDENCE

Support & sourcing

Claims are checked for proportional evidence, citation placement, and unsupported leaps in reasoning.

DIM · REGISTER

Scholarly voice

Hedging calibration, nominalization balance, and the removal of conversational or promotional tone.

DIM · INTEGRITY

Rigor flags

Overclaiming, vague attributions, missing limitations, and structural gaps are flagged before an examiner finds them.

Built to be checked

Trust is engineered, not promised.

Every score is auditable, every rule is public, and your thesis belongs to you — always.

Blind, versioned examiner

Each round is judged fresh — the examiner never sees prior scores or that text was corrected. Every report is stamped Examiner v2.0 and carries its ±3–4 variance band, so a score means the same thing next month.

The public rubric

90–100 publication-ready · 80–89 strong, minor polish · 70–79 competent with clear issues · 55–69 weak · below 55 deficient. Eleven dimensions across four boards — the same standard, every run.

Your thesis is yours

Private by default, never shared, never used to train models. Export everything or delete your account — and everything in it — at any time.

What it will never do

Invent sources. Fabricate data. Attach citations without your approval. Inflate a score. The tool prepares you for examination — it does not deceive one. A per-chapter AI-assistance disclosure statement is one click away.
